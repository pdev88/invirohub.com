ABOUT US
The Invirohub Skill Development approach is based on the Invirohub partners FET Colleges Internship Programme. This programme was initiated in early 2011 out of a realisation that there is a death of skills in the ICT sector, particularly in the Black community. This skills shortage had a direct impact of inhibiting organisation development, infrastructure roll-out and sustainable transformation. The programme was started with Ekurhuleni East FET College as a partner and has since expanded to 6 partner colleges in Limpopo and Gauteng provinces, and soon expanding into Kwa Zulu - Natal. The programme was initiated with ICT Seta and is now executed in partnership with the National Skills Fund at DHET.

Invirohub Skill Development through its partners is responding to the president’s appeal to absorb FET graduates who are awaiting placements. In his state of national address, the South African president said, “State owned companies provide apprenticeships and learnerships and we urge that these be increased.  We appeal to the private sector to absorb 11 000 FET graduates who are awaiting placements”.

We have highly skilled electricians and civil engineers who will assist in training these unemployed FET College graduates. The practical and on the job training offered to these FET graduates will assist them in obtaining a National Diploma after 2 years of training. We will provide induction and focus more on On-The-Job training with qualified supervisors who will be able to coach and mentor simultaneously.

This approach has worked wonderfully with learners from the Ekurhuleni East College, Sekhukune FET College, and George Tabor FET Colleges. We have trained and deployed over 50 FET college students between 2011 and 2012. We currently have over 40 students on a learnership with us, whereby same approach is applied. Our students have after the programme received job opportunities with the likes of Huawei and other companies in our industry.

The ultimate legacy of the programme will not only be measured by the technical and commercial outcomes, but also on what positive impacts the programme will have on marginalised communities in South Africa. The creation of skills development and employment opportunities for Unemployed Youth in the programme is key to ensuring that those who will ultimately inherit the system have a sense of pride and ownership thereof.
